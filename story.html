How I got diagnosed, what I've been through, and why I decided to start talking about it.

What I've Learned

Understanding what endometriosis actually does inside my body helped me fight for better care.

If you're reading this and you're going through something similar, here's what I wish someone had told me:

Your pain is real. Even if doctors dismiss it. Even if your family doesn't understand. Even if you're tired of explaining it. It's real, and you're not overreacting.

You don't have to accept bad treatment. If a doctor isn't listening to you, find another one. If a medication is making things worse, speak up. You have the right to ask questions, to say no, and to demand better care.

It's okay to be angry. About the years it took to get diagnosed. About the unnecessary procedures. About the lack of research funding because this is a "women's issue." Be angry. Use that anger to advocate for yourself.

Your mental health matters too. The depression and anxiety aren't separate from your chronic illness ‚Äî they're part of it. Take them just as seriously as the physical pain.

You're allowed to rest. Cancel plans. Say no. Stay in bed when you need to. Rest is not laziness. Rest is survival.

You're not alone. Even when it feels like no one gets it, there are other women out there fighting the same fight. Find them. Talk to them. We need each other.

Why I Started This Blog

I started writing because I needed somewhere to put all of this. The frustration, the pain, the small victories, the bad days. Somewhere that felt honest.

If my story helps even one person feel less alone, or helps someone recognize their own symptoms earlier, or gives someone the courage to push back against a dismissive doctor ‚Äî then it's worth it.

This is my story. But it doesn't have to be yours. We deserve better care, better research, and better understanding. And we're going to keep talking until we get it.
